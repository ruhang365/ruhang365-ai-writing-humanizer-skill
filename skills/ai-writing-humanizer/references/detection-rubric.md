# Detection Rubric

Use this for long drafts, high-stakes public copy, or when the user asks for a detailed AI-flavor audit.

## Six Buckets

### 1. Material Gaps

- Generic people, generic scenes, generic examples.
- Numbers, timelines, quotes, or "case studies" without a source.
- Advice that names a concept but never shows an action.
- Stories without a name, mistake, constraint, or concrete moment.

### 2. Slippery Judgment

- Every side is treated as equally correct.
- The conclusion refuses to choose.
- Strong tone substitutes for evidence.
- Universal protocol language appears after a nuanced setup.
- Costs, boundaries, and "not for this situation" statements are missing.

### 3. Mechanical Structure

- Over-neat openings such as "随着..." or "在当今...".
- Repeated first/second/finally transitions.
- Three-part slogans and mirrored paragraph shapes.
- Every paragraph ends with a mini-summary or golden sentence.
- Sentence lengths stay too even for too long.

### 4. Reader Overreach

- The author tells readers what they "must be thinking" without evidence.
- "Many people think X, actually Y" appears when X is not a real reader belief.
- Self-Q&A creates a fake classroom where the author controls both question and answer.
- The draft performs insight by correcting a reader it has invented.

### 5. Floating Language

- Abstract benefits without actions: improve efficiency, build systems, optimize expression.
- Inflated labels: empower, closed loop, underlying logic, cognitive upgrade, key lever, value precipitation.
- Vague attribution: experts say, reports show, some observers believe.
- Chat residue: great question, hope this helps, let's dive in.

### 6. Formatting Tells

- Bullet points all use the same "keyword: explanation" mold.
- Dividers, bold text, and dashes create a generated template feel.
- Mixed Chinese/English spacing is inconsistent.
- The ending sounds like customer service instead of an author stopping at the right point.

## Judgment Rule

Do not mark a pattern as wrong just because it appears. Mark it when it harms the current format, author intent, target reader, or credibility.
