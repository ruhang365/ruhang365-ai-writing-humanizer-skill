# False Positives

Do not flatten useful human writing just because it is clean, technical, formal, or structured. A pattern is actionable only when it harms the current format, author intent, target reader, credibility, or platform nativeness.

## Not reliable indicators on their own

- **Perfect grammar and consistent style**: many people write cleanly or edit carefully.
- **Formal vocabulary**: professional language is not AI flavor. Only flag specific padded terms when they replace material or judgment.
- **Technical terms**: MCP, API, workflow, token, runtime, manifest, and similar terms can be necessary.
- **Common transition words**: "此外", "因此", "however", "moreover" are only suspicious when piled up.
- **A single dash, bold phrase, emoji, or heading**: formatting alone does not prove AI flavor.
- **Curly quotes or punctuation style**: editors and operating systems often create these.
- **One short emphatic sentence**: humans use short lines. Flag only stacked, inflated drama.
- **A clean list**: checklists can be platform-native on Xiaohongshu, WeChat, training material, SOP docs, and product guides.
- **Unsourced claims**: lack of citation is a credibility issue, not automatic AI evidence. Ask whether the claim needs sourcing for this format.
- **Dry prose**: boring writing is not automatically AI writing. AI writing has clusters of specific tells.

## Human signals to preserve

- **Hard-to-fabricate detail**: dates, prices, filenames, screenshots, exact workflow steps, constraints, failed attempts, product names, and edge cases.
- **Mixed feelings**: "有用，但我不建议直接上生产", "这个变化好，但也会制造新问题".
- **Defensible first-person choices**: "我会先看这个指标", "这类场景我不会这么做".
- **Platform-native compression**: X, short-video scripts, and cover copy may need sharp, compressed rhythm.
- **Genuine asides or self-corrections**: short parenthetical thoughts can be a real voice signal.
- **Dated or subculture-specific references**: memes, slang, and community language can signal a real author, if they fit.
- **Useful repetition**: repeating the same technical term can be clearer than synonym cycling.
- **Strong position with boundary**: a clear claim is not AI flavor when it states the cost, limitation, or evidence.

## Do not over-correct into these failures

- Making every sentence casual.
- Making every judgment uncertain.
- Replacing the author's voice with generic professional voice.
- Adding fake vulnerability, fake slang, fake stories, fake data, fake quotes, or fake client feedback.
- Turning every list into prose when the platform rewards saveable structure.
- Removing brand or product names when they are the content object.
- Removing all formatting from a platform that needs scanability.
- Translating technical terms into awkward Chinese when the audience already uses the original term.
- Rewriting a narrow true claim into a broader unsupported claim.

## Source and chronology caveat

- Text written before November 30, 2022 is usually not ChatGPT-generated. Do not apply modern AI-detection assumptions blindly.
- Human editors, CMS templates, and brand style guides can create formatting patterns that resemble AI output.
- Treat "AI flavor" as a writing-quality diagnosis, not an authorship accusation.

## Decision rule

Before editing, answer:

1. What concrete harm does this pattern create here?
2. Is it part of a cluster?
3. Does removing it preserve the author's material, judgment, and voice?
4. Would the target platform expect this shape?
5. Are we fixing AI flavor, or just enforcing our taste?

If the answer is unclear, leave the sentence alone or mark it as a suggestion, not a blocker.
