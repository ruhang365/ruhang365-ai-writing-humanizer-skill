# Formatting Checks

Use this when the text is public-facing Chinese copy or when the draft still looks generated after rewriting.

## Chinese Readability

- Keep paragraph length varied. Avoid making every paragraph 1-2 similar sentences.
- Use headings that say something, not generic labels.
- Avoid overusing bold, separators, arrows, and decorative symbols.
- End when the argument is complete. Do not add "hope this helps" style endings.

## Mixed Chinese And English

- Keep spacing consistent around English words, numbers, and units.
- Preserve standard product names and technical terms.
- Avoid translationese such as "作为", "关于", "基于", "进行" when simpler verbs work.

## Lists

- Lists should not all use the same mold.
- Use a list when scanning matters; use paragraphs when reasoning matters.
- Each list item should contain an action, judgment, constraint, or example, not just a noun.

## Rhythm

- Human writing usually has uneven rhythm: short sentence, longer explanation, pause, turn.
- Do not cut every sentence short.
- Do not make every paragraph end with a punchline.
