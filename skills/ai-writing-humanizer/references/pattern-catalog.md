# Pattern Catalog

This catalog maps the useful checks from generic Humanizer v2.8.0 into the `Material / Judgment / Voice` model for Chinese public writing. It is a diagnostic map, not a word blacklist.

Use it this way:

1. Look for clusters, not isolated words.
2. Ask what the pattern damages: credibility, reader fit, author voice, platform nativeness, factual boundary, or action value.
3. Delete correct-but-empty sentences before polishing them.
4. Replace abstraction with material, judgment, or voice.
5. Preserve useful roughness and platform-native compression.

## A. Material problems

These patterns usually mean the draft lacks real material.

1. **Significance inflation**
   - English source pattern: inflated legacy, broader trends, pivotal moments.
   - Chinese tell: "标志着重大转折", "开启新时代", "重塑行业格局", "具有重要意义", "深刻改变".
   - Fix: name the actual change, actor, date, scope, constraint, or user impact.

2. **Notability name-dropping**
   - Tell: listing media names, follower counts, awards, or "被多家媒体报道" without explaining what it proves.
   - Fix: keep one relevant source or cut the proof-by-name.

3. **Superficial present-participle analysis**
   - English source pattern: "highlighting/reflecting/showcasing..." padding.
   - Chinese tell: "这也体现了", "进一步说明了", "展现出", "折射出" followed by a generic point.
   - Fix: replace the symbolic reading with a concrete fact or cut it.

4. **Promotional language**
   - Tell: "重磅", "震撼", "革命性", "极致体验", "全新升级", "值得每个人关注".
   - Fix: state the feature, limitation, and actual use case.

5. **Vague authority**
   - Tell: "专家认为", "行业报告显示", "外界普遍认为", "不少观察者指出".
   - Fix: name the source, downgrade to observation, or remove.

6. **Formulaic challenges section**
   - Tell: "虽然面临挑战，但未来依然广阔", "机遇与挑战并存".
   - Fix: say which problem blocks adoption, who is affected, and what decision changes.

7. **Knowledge-cutoff and speculative gap filling**
   - Tell: "截至目前资料有限", "可能成长于", "据推测", "保持低调", "相关信息并不公开" followed by plausible filler.
   - Fix: say the information is unavailable or omit the paragraph. Do not invent biography, customer cases, metrics, quotes, or source links.

8. **Unsupported personal-feeling simulation**
   - Tell: "我亲眼见过很多人", "我们团队踩过这个坑", "客户反馈说" when the user did not provide it.
   - Fix: remove, or mark as a hypothetical scenario.

## B. Judgment problems

These patterns usually mean the draft avoids a real position.

9. **AI vocabulary as judgment substitute**
   - Tell: "赋能", "底层逻辑", "认知升级", "闭环", "抓手", "价值沉淀", "生态", "范式迁移".
   - Fix: name the decision, risk, tradeoff, or next action.

10. **Copula avoidance and inflated verbs**
    - English source pattern: "serves as", "stands as", "boasts".
    - Chinese tell: "作为一种重要载体", "承载着", "展现出", "构建起".
    - Fix: use plain verbs: 是、有、能、不能、适合、不适合.

11. **Negative parallelism overuse**
    - Tell: "不是 X，而是 Y", "不只是 X，更是 Y", "它不是工具，是镜子".
    - Fix: keep it only when X is a real misconception. Otherwise say the claim directly.

12. **Tailing negations and slogan fragments**
    - English source pattern: clipped fragments like "no guessing".
    - Chinese tell: "不绕路", "不废话", "不踩坑" tacked onto a sentence without material.
    - Fix: turn the fragment into a real action or delete it.

13. **Rule-of-three padding**
    - Tell: every point becomes three nouns, three benefits, or three steps.
    - Fix: keep the natural number of points. Two strong details beat three padded ones.

14. **Elegant variation / synonym cycling**
    - Tell: switching terms for the same object to avoid repetition: 工具/系统/平台/方案/能力 all referring to one thing.
    - Fix: use the stable term when it improves clarity.

15. **False ranges**
    - Tell: "从个人到组织，从工具到生态，从效率到增长" when these are not real endpoints.
    - Fix: list the actual covered cases.

16. **Passive voice and subjectless fragments**
    - English source pattern: actorless lines like "No configuration file needed."
    - Chinese tell: "无需配置", "已完成升级", "自动沉淀结果" without who does what.
    - Fix: add the actor when it helps: 用户、系统、团队、发布者、审核人.

17. **Persuasive authority tropes**
    - Tell: "真正的问题是", "归根到底", "本质上", "核心在于", "说到底".
    - Fix: use only when the next sentence gives a sharper claim, not a ceremony.

18. **Aphorism formulas**
    - Tell: "X 是 Y 的语言", "X 是 Z 的镜子", "效率会变成陷阱", "信任的货币".
    - Fix: translate the metaphor into a concrete claim.

## C. Voice problems

These patterns usually mean the draft has a generated rhythm.

19. **Mechanical rhythm**
    - Tell: paragraphs are same length, same turn, same mini-summary.
    - Fix: vary paragraph length and let some sections end cleanly without recap.

20. **Manufactured punchlines**
    - Tell: every sentence tries to land as a quote.
    - Fix: reserve short lines for real emphasis; use ordinary prose elsewhere.

21. **Staccato drama**
    - Tell: a stack of clipped lines: "规则变了。边界没了。机会来了。"
    - Fix: merge into one precise sentence unless the platform specifically needs the rhythm.

22. **Fake-candid openers**
    - Tell: "说实话", "老实说", "你有没有发现", "很多人以为", "Honestly?", "Look".
    - Fix: start with the actual observation. Keep the opener only if it matches the author's real voice.

23. **Conversational rhetorical opener**
    - Tell: one-word question plus reveal: "值得吗？说实话？看情况。"
    - Fix: remove the staged pause.

24. **Soulless neutrality**
    - Tell: clean, correct, balanced, but no author choice or emotional range.
    - Fix: add a defensible judgment, uncertainty, or boundary when the format allows it.

25. **Voice overwrite**
    - Tell: the rewrite becomes polished but stops sounding like the author.
    - Fix: restore the author's useful rhythm, recurring words, and attitude.

## D. Formatting and presentation problems

These patterns usually mean the draft looks generated even before reading closely.

26. **Dash habit**
    - English source pattern: em dash / en dash / double hyphen as AI rhythm.
    - Chinese mapping: repeated dash, colon, parenthesis, or broken-line asides used to manufacture drama.
    - Fix: use a sentence, comma, colon, or parentheses only when it clarifies. For English final copy, scan for em/en dashes.

27. **Boldface overuse**
    - Tell: every key phrase is bolded.
    - Fix: use structure and content to carry emphasis. Keep bold only where the platform needs scanability.

28. **Inline-header vertical lists**
    - Tell: every bullet starts with "关键词：解释".
    - Fix: keep for saveable checklists; otherwise vary the shape or write prose.

29. **Title-case heading habit**
    - English source pattern: every word capitalized.
    - Chinese mapping: headings sound like template modules: "核心洞察", "行动建议", "总结升华".
    - Fix: write headings that match the platform and reader task.

30. **Emoji decoration**
    - Tell: emoji used as section furniture rather than meaning.
    - Fix: remove unless platform-native and sparse.

31. **Curly quote / punctuation artifacts**
    - English source pattern: curly quotes as ChatGPT residue.
    - Chinese mapping: mixed full/half width punctuation, inconsistent Chinese-English spacing, copied chat formatting.
    - Fix: normalize only when it improves readability. Do not treat punctuation alone as proof.

32. **Fragmented headers**
    - Tell: heading followed by a one-line warm-up that repeats the heading.
    - Fix: start the section with material.

33. **Diff-anchored writing**
    - Tell: "这次新增了", "相比之前", "本文更新了" in evergreen docs or posts where the reader does not need commit context.
    - Fix: describe the current thing as it is. Keep version language only in changelogs or release notes.

## E. Communication residue

These are usually copied from chat output into public copy.

34. **Signposting and announcements**
    - Tell: "下面我们来拆解", "让我们开始", "接下来你会看到", "here is what you need to know".
    - Fix: do the work instead of announcing the work.

35. **Collaborative chatbot artifacts**
    - Tell: "当然可以", "希望对你有帮助", "如果你需要我可以继续", "要不要我再给你".
    - Fix: remove from public copy.

36. **Sycophantic tone**
    - Tell: "这个问题问得非常好", "你说得完全对", "这是一个很有价值的方向".
    - Fix: answer the point directly.

37. **Filler phrases**
    - Tell: "为了实现这一目标", "在这个过程中", "需要注意的是", "从某种程度上来说".
    - Fix: shorten or cut.

38. **Excessive hedging**
    - Tell: "可能会在一定程度上产生某种影响".
    - Fix: choose the actual level of certainty.

39. **Generic positive conclusion**
    - Tell: "未来值得期待", "让我们拭目以待", "这只是开始".
    - Fix: end with a useful action, risk, decision, or clean stop.

40. **Hyphenated word-pair overuse**
    - English source pattern: uniformly hyphenated compounds.
    - Chinese mapping: stacks of compound labels: "数据驱动、端到端、全链路、跨职能、实时化".
    - Fix: keep labels that describe a real mechanism; cut decorative stacks.

## F. Chinese social-media tells

41. **Fake saveability**
    - Tell: "收藏这一篇就够了" without a real checklist, template, or decision tree.
    - Fix: provide the checklist or remove the claim.

42. **Ordinary people promise**
    - Tell: "普通人也能..." followed by traffic, income, offer, or transformation implication.
    - Fix: state the realistic condition and limitation.

43. **Hard-sell CTA**
    - Tell: repeated brand push, private-contact hints, or platform-forbidden diversion.
    - Fix: make the CTA a natural continuation of the content.

44. **Fake classroom control**
    - Tell: the author invents the reader's question and then wins the argument.
    - Fix: use a real objection, or frame it as a possible question.

45. **Final quote ritual**
    - Tell: "最后送你一句话" followed by generic slogan.
    - Fix: end where the useful thought ends.
